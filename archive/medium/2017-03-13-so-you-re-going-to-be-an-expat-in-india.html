So You’re Going to be an Expat in India?

By Matt Everard · March 13, 2017

On March 31, 2015, we arrived in Pune on a two-year secondment (Brit fancy term for “gig”) and a massive amount of learning. As we wrap up our time here, I’ve captured some Do’s and Don’ts for newbies:

  • There is no “3 second” food-on-ground rule here — if it hits the floor, put it in the bin
  • Your hands are dirty. Always. Don’t rub your eye, pick your nose, or teeth
  • Let sleeping dogs lie — literally you will understand this saying in a new way
  • Smuggle in as much booze and wine as you can fit in your luggage on every trip out of country
  • Stay heads down at customs, move quickly, quietly say, “These are not the droids you are looking for”
  • Bring cured meat and cheese in your luggage along with wine. You laugh now, but once here, you’ll see the genius of it
  • Throw parties with other expats when you return with those Iberian hams and Priorat wine. You will be much loved
  • Watch Million Dollar Arm
  • Read Shantaram (listen haters — it’s a good book)
  • Your driver does not work on Sunday (even though his company says he does)
  • You will overpay your maid — and your stay-at-home spouse will undoubtedly complain about her
  • Make a family rule: No complaining about the maid
  • Don’t eat out “like a local”- you are not a local
  • Contrary to popular wisdom, our intestines did *not* acclimate in 2 years
  • Unless you can see the ocean, skip the seafood
  • Always buy VIP movie tickets (like first class airplane seats)
  • Never stay at 1–3 star hotels
  • Get a water cooler on day 1
  • Don’t drink the RO water (no minerals)
  • No returns allowed — once purchased, it is yours. Period. No matter what they tell you
  • Everything can be delivered
  • Buy meat from the Hyatt — not local grocery stores (single-chain of refrigeration custody)
  • Expect to spend way more for western quality in every category of goods
  • Scan all your important docs and put them on Dropbox before you leave — you will be shocked at the amount of paperwork involved in your stay
  • Get a scanning app for your pocket computer
  • Bring your own BBQ — even if you grill irregularly (they are insanely expensive here)
  • Set aside a weekend to procure your gas cylinder (it is considered a weapon)
  • Pack warm clothes for holiday trips
  • Bring racquets and golf clubs (but that’s about all for sporting equip)
  • Buy unlocked iPhone on Amazon here (not in the mall)
  • Buy everything at Amazon. Avoid the malls
  • Get Hotsar (select HBO titles for $4/mo)
  • Pack Sonos speakers (220V compatible)
  • Bring good kitchen tools
  • Clothes are cheaper and better at a Banana Republic sale in the States
  • You never walk anywhere — sidewalks (when present) are motorbike lines
  • Good chocolate is rare
  • Daily supply of protein is a constant struggle (would you like chicken or chicken?)
  • Don’t get a dog
  • Consider buying an Enfield
  • Refresh your mac hardware before leaving
  • Buy Phillips air purifiers
  • Give your driver extra money — he’s living on $200USD per month
  • Tip as often and as generously as you want. Everyone in food service is broke (living on less than your driver)
