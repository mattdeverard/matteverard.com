Q: "So tell me how did you decide to move to Vancouver"

In order to answer that one well, I have to take you on a little journey. Are you ready? I'll try to keep it brief.

After grad school in Chicago, I came back to Columbus to be close to family (raising babies = lots of work) and start a company. I can tell you lots of stories about the kids later. Right now, I'll stick to the business.

In 1997 I co-founded a software development company with a buddy from KPMG. He managed the money, and I drove the tech side of the house. We grew our business to over $1M in revenue in less than 3 years, serving volatile flame-outs like Restaurant.com, the more pedestrian BN.com, and the rock solid, "always pays their bills" clients like The Kroger Co. We had a great time along the way and built a team of passionate, talented people. We kept our team small, maintained a family-friendly environment, and resisted the insanity prevalent in the late 90s.

That helped us survive the dot-bomb shakeout, and the company is still a going concern. Unfortunately starting in 2004, the fun project work dried up and our team had to be parceled out to sit at customer sites 5 days a week. That was the beginning of the end for me. Don't get me wrong--it's a profitable business, but all the fun had gone. It was time for me to make a change. My partner was very generous with me, giving me lots of time to figure out what to do next. I ended up going on a long vacation to New Zealand with my wife and sons. What better way to live out your severance than on the beach?

When I returned to Columbus, it was the middle of winter and it was cold and ugly. Coming off the plane I knew what had to be done--we had to move to the coast. At that time I thought it would be NYC--I love the energy and international culture of the Big Apple. Plus, I love brownstones, public transportation, carryout, and liquor stores that stay open until all hours of the night.

A few months later I was skiing at Kicking Horse in the BC interior, and gazing out over the peaks exclaimed, "Damn, I really love the mountains!" My good friends on the trip (who live in Vancouver) sensed my weakness and began an all-out marketing assult. It took a few months, but now my wife and I are sold. The kids are sold. The house is rented. Hell, I've even turned down job offers in NYC (still stings a little).

I decided to do short-term consulting in Columbus as a Program/Project Manager and network my way into a job in Vancouver. If you are reading this, you are proof that my marketing genius is working.

Drop me a note. I love meeting new people.
